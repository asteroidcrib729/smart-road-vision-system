I Have Added A New Directory To The Project Named "smart-road-vision-system-smart-road-vision-system-refactor". This Directory Contains A Newer Version Of The Vehicle Tracking Code That Uses TransReID For Vehicle Re-Identification And It Also Contains ANPR And Helmet Detection Code In The Form Of Callable Modules. We Have Already Sorted Some Of The Primary Issues That We Were Facing In Terms Of Reading Bike Number-Plates Or Detecting Helmets And The Time To Sort Out The Entire Backend Of Smart Road Vision System Has Come. The Entire Backend Could Be Desribed As A Sequential Process With The Following Sequence:

1) Take Two Video Feeds As Input. One Contains Footage Of Incoming Traffic And The Front-Side Of Vehicles Will Appear Clearly In This Footage. This Front-Side Footage Will Be Used For Tracking, Identifying, And Reading Number-Plates Of Large Vehicles Starting From Cars, Trucks, Buses, Vans, Rickshaws, Utility Vehicles, Law Enforcement Vehicles, Etecetera. Since None These Vehicles Require The Driver To Wear A Helmet, Running Helmet Detection Code On These Vehicles Will Not Be Necessary. Then, There Will Be Another Video Feed And This Will Contain Footage Of Outgoing Traffic And The Rear-Side Of Vehicles Will Appear Clearly In This Footage. This Rear-Side Footage Will Be Strictly Used For Reading Bike Number-Plates And Identifying If A Biker Is Wearing A Helmet Or Not. Now, I Need You To Move Forward With The Assumption That We Are Processing Two Video Feeds For Tracking Vehicles And Reading Numberplates And Create Input Variables With Placeholders Because I Do Not Have Access To The Footage At The Moment.

2) Processing The Video Feeds As Input Is The Very First Step Of This Process. The Second Step Is To Conduct Frame-By-Frame Analysis Of The Video Feeds For Tracking Individual Vehicles Across All Frames They Are Appearing In And Combining All Those Frames To Capture Their One Best Snapshot With Highest Visual Detail Which Can Be Used By On-Foot Traffic Cops For Identifying The Vehicles In-Person And For Reading The Number-Plates And Storing Them In A CSV. Once Again, The Tracking Process For Bikes Is To Be Kept Separate From Other Vehicles Based On The Two Distinct Video Feeds Where We Are Using A Front-Side Footage For Large Vehicles Only With The Exemption Of Bikes, And A Rear-Side Footage For Bikes With The Exemption Of Other Types/Classes Of Vehicles.

3) The Third Step Is To Use Those Vehicle Tracking Details Captured Across All Frames The Individual Vehicles Appeared In And Use Them To Identify If The Individual Vehicles Are Indulging In Any Kind Of Traffic Violations Such As Unreadable/Missing License Plates And Over-Speeding.

4) The Fourth Step Requires The Implementation Of A Fall-Back Mechanism That Ensures If YOLO/PaddleOCR Approach Fails When Reading A Vehicle's Number-Plate Or Detecting A Biker's Helmet, We Can Prompt The API-Based Approaches From "Extract_Numberplates.ipynb" And "Extract_Helmets.ipynb" In The Form Of Callable Modules.

5) The Fifth Step Is A Part Of When We Are Tracking Vehicles As Well. Each Vehicle Must Be Assigned A Unique Tracking ID And This Tracking ID Should Be Stored As The Primary Identification Number While The Read Number-Plate Is To Be Kept Secondary Since It Is A Possibility That Some Vehicles May Or May Not Have A Readable Number-Plate Or Any Number-Plate At All. Furthermore, Strict De-Duplication Logic Must Be Implemented To Ensure One Vehicle Does Not Get Captured Multiple Times With Different Tracking IDs.

6) Snapshots For Each Vehicle Type/Class Must Be Stored In Separate Directories In This Exact Format: f"{Tracking_ID}.jpg". Assigned Tracking IDs And Read Number-Plates Must Be Stored In Separate CSV Files For Each Vehicle Type/Class. For Bikes And Bikes In Particular, The CSV File Must Contain Four Fields Such As "Assigned Tracking ID Number", "Read Number-Plate", "Helmet Detected" In The Form Of Yes Or No, "Violation Detected" In The Form Of Yes Or No. For Other Larger Vehicles "Helmet Detected" Field Will Not Be Applicable So There Separate CSV Files Based On Their Distinct Type/Class May Only Contain Three Fields Such As "Assigned Tracking ID Number", "Read Number-Plate", "Violation Detected" In The Form Of Yes Or No.

7) At The End, After A Vehicle's Number-Plate Has Been Appropriately Detected And Read Or A Biker's Helmet Has Been Detected And Identified Correctly, I Need You To Prompt The API-Based Approach From "Enhanced_Image_Generation_Real-ESRGAN.ipynb" For Restoring Bike Snapshots To Improve The Visual Details Such That These Snapshots Can Be Displayed On A Final Dashboard Or For Traffic Cops To Identify These Bikes On-Foot In-Person. The Restored Bike Snapshots Must Be Stored In Separate Directory In This Specific Format: f"Restored_{Tracking_ID}.jpg"
